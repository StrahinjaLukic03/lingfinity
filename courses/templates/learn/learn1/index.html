Pronouns

In Finnish there are personal pronouns just as in almost any other language. One special thing in Finnish language is that it has one pronoun for both women and men, which is hän ((s)he).


Finnish English
Minä I
Sinä You (singular)
Hän (S)he
Me We
Te You (plural)
He They

Here are some use cases for the pronouns in the table below. It’s important to use correct verb forms when making sentences with pronouns.


Finnish English
Minä olen iloinen. I am happy.
Sinä kävelet. You are walking. (singular)
Hän nauraa. (S)he is laughing.
Me olemme lääkäreitä. We are doctors.
Te tanssitte. You are dancing. (plural)
He ovat lapsia. They are children.

When you know how to use correct verb forms, you can try doing it the spoken way.

In spoken language people are often referred to as se which means it. In formal occasions it’s good to use hän to sound polite.

Minä (I) and sinä (you) can also be shortened to mä and sä. When using mä and sä in a sentence, the verb form also shortens (Minä olen → mä oon) (Sinä olet → sä oot).

He (they) is also often changed to ne in spoken language. The verb also changes to basic form (He ovat → Ne on).

When using me (we) in a sentence, the verb changes to passive form (Me olemme → Me ollaan).
